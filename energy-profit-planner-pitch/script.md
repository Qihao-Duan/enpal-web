Imagine a medium-sized factory with a large solar rooftop.

---

At noon, it produces more solar energy than it can use.

---

Without an intelligent planning layer, that surplus often gets sold to the grid at a low price.

---

Then later, when machines, forklifts, cooling systems, and batteries need power, the factory buys electricity back at a higher price.

---

So the problem is not only energy supply.

---

The real question is: where should every kilowatt-hour go next?

---

Our project is Energy Profit Planner.

---

It connects to the factory's energy profile: solar panels, batteries, EVs, production equipment, heating, cooling, and historical consumption.

---

It also reads contract terms, dynamic prices, weather forecasts, and device specifications.

---

From that, it creates an optimized 24-hour energy plan, broken into short time intervals.

---

If solar generation peaks at noon, the system can charge batteries and electric forklifts during that window.

---

If prices rise later, it can save stored energy for expensive periods.

---

If the market price is attractive, it can recommend selling surplus energy instead of storing it.

---

The user does not see raw energy data.

---

They see a direct comparison: normal cost, optimized cost, and how much money they can save or earn.

---

We offer two service levels.

---

The basic plan focuses on smart charging and appliance scheduling, so regular users reduce cost without changing daily routines.

---

The premium plan adds solar trading and storage optimization for factories, solar households, and battery-heavy sites.

---

That is the shift: we do not just monitor energy consumption.

---

We plan energy usage and energy sales.

---

Every kilowatt-hour moves to the place where it creates the most economic value.
